# ADR-004: Separate Real, Calibrated, Synthetic, and Simulated Evidence

## Purpose
Record the evidence-separation decision.

## Background
Multiple reviews identified mixed evidence classes as a scientific, product, and adoption risk.

## Current State
SNTO contains real, calibrated, synthetic, and simulated evidence paths. This transparency is valuable, but operational users must never confuse them.

## Evidence
The scientific review, protected-area director review, product review, and consensus analysis all repeated this concern.

## Problem
If evidence classes are visually or semantically blended, SNTO may overstate certainty and lose institutional trust.

## Context
Protected-area decisions can affect public access, budgets, and conservation priorities. Evidence provenance must be unambiguous.

## Options Considered
- Preserve evidence labels only in methodology documentation.
- Show evidence labels only in audit screens.
- Make evidence class a first-class product and data concept.

## Decision
Real, calibrated, synthetic, and simulated evidence must be separated as first-class concepts in data, UI, reports, and documentation.

## Consequences
Positive: stronger scientific credibility and lower adoption risk. Negative: product screens may initially feel less polished because uncertainty is more visible.

## Status
Accepted from 2026 consensus.
