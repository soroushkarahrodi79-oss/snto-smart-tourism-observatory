# Consensus Analysis

## Purpose
Identify repeated recommendations, unique recommendations, conflicts, high-confidence conclusions, low-confidence conclusions, and validation needs.

## Background
The consensus engine combines all approved review perspectives.

## Current State
The strongest agreement is that SNTO has a differentiated thesis but must narrow, validate, operationalize, and harden before claiming reference status.

## Evidence
Repeated recommendations:
- Position as protected-area decision intelligence.
- Build around asset risk -> confidence -> action -> budget.
- Validate with field evidence.
- Add enterprise auth, audit, tenancy, and observability.
- Separate real, calibrated, synthetic, and simulated evidence.
- Integrate with GIS platforms rather than replacing them.

Unique recommendations:
- CTO: reject enterprise customers until platform foundations exist.
- Scientific board: avoid causal overclaiming until validation.
- CPO: package as paid pilot and avoid horizontal competition.
- Director: prioritize daily decision brief and field verification.

Conflicting opinions:
- Product review saw paid pilot potential sooner than CTO enterprise acceptance.
- Director saw operational value before scientific validation is complete.
- Scientific review supported cautious pilots but rejected authoritative claims.

Highest-confidence conclusions:
- SNTO's core innovation is the evidence-to-decision chain.
- Enterprise readiness is currently insufficient.
- Scientific validation is the main credibility gate.
- Product focus must narrow.

Lowest-confidence conclusions:
- Exact pricing potential.
- International adoption probability.
- Generalizability across habitats and governance contexts.

Topics requiring further validation:
- Field accuracy.
- Causal attribution.
- Indicator calibration.
- Multi-territory transferability.
- Institutional willingness to pay.

## Recommendations
Use consensus findings as the highest-priority strategic input.

## Next Steps
Review consensus annually and update probabilities after pilots.
